You are a Senior UX Architect and UI Designer working on a digital self-service product.

Using the attached BRD and FSD documents as your source of truth, generate a comprehensive 
UI/UX Specification Document for the App Adoption (DIY Servicing Feature).

The document must include:

1. **UX Goals & Design Principles**
   - Map each principle to a business objective from the BRD

2. **User Personas** (derive from BRD actors/user stories)
   - Name, role, goals, pain points, tech comfort level

3. **Information Architecture**
   - Site/App map showing all screens and their hierarchy

4. **User Flow Diagrams** (mermaid)
   - One flow per functional requirement (FR-01 to FR-05)
   - Include happy path + error/alternate paths

5. **Wireframe Descriptions** (screen-by-screen)
   - Screen name, purpose, key UI components, interactions, 
     validations, and empty/error states
   - Cover: Login, Service Catalogue, Submit Request, 
     Guided Troubleshooting, Request Tracking

6. **Component Specifications**
   - Buttons, forms, cards, modals, loaders, notifications
   - Behaviour on mobile vs web

7. **Notification UX**
   - Push and email notification trigger points and copy tone

8. **Accessibility & Responsiveness Notes**
   - WCAG compliance level, breakpoints, touch targets

9. **Prototype Annotations**
   - Clickable prototype flow notes (what links to what)

10. **Traceability**
    - Map each screen/flow back to FR-01 to FR-05 from the FSD

Format the output as a structured document with clear section headers. 
Use tables where applicable. Flag any UX decisions that require 
stakeholder sign-off as [DECISION NEEDED].
